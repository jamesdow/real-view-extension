Detection

Visual AI Detection on-device, experimental

Runs a real machine-learning model against the actual pixels of images the free signals above couldn't classify (domain lists and metadata don't help with, say, an AI-generated illustration hosted on a legitimate news site). Everything runs on your device — nothing is ever uploaded. Downloads a ~320MB model the first time you turn this on (cached after), and uses noticeably more CPU per image. Only classifies images actually visible on screen, never a whole page's backlog at once.

Model: haywoodsloan/ai-image-detector-deploy — community-sourced, no license listed. Treat this as experimental, not a guarantee.

Expect it to be wrong sometimes. In our own testing (a hand-built 25-image set, not an independent benchmark), this model correctly called roughly 84% of images. It's tuned to stay quiet rather than guess — an image it isn't confident about is left alone, never falsely flagged as AI just to make a call either way.

A newer version of the visual model is available.
